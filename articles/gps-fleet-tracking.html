How GPS Fleet Tracking Saved One Accra Business GHS 12,000 Last Year

February 28, 2025 4 min read BESITS Team
Back to Blog

Running a logistics or transport business in Greater Accra is tough. Fuel prices fluctuate, traffic is unpredictable, and keeping close oversight of a fleet spread across Tema, Accra, Kasoa, and beyond can feel impossible. One of our clients — a mid-sized logistics company operating eight delivery vehicles — knows this reality all too well. What changed everything for them was a decision to install GPS vehicle trackers on their entire fleet through BESITS. Twelve months later, the numbers told a striking story.

The Problem

Before installing GPS tracking, the company's operations manager had a nagging suspicion that something was wrong with their fuel budget. Month after month, fuel costs were running 20–25% higher than industry benchmarks for a fleet their size. Deliveries were occasionally late with no clear explanation, and a few clients had started to raise concerns about inconsistencies in service.

When the manager started asking questions, several patterns emerged:

  • Fuel siphoning: Drivers were refuelling at company expense and siphoning excess fuel to sell privately.
  • Unauthorised trips: Vehicles were being used for private errands in the evenings and on weekends — running up mileage and fuel costs the business never sanctioned.
  • Route inefficiency: Without oversight, some drivers were taking longer routes, likely to clock more hours or avoid traffic, at the company's cost.
  • False mileage reports: Log books were being falsified to conceal the extra kilometres.

The manager had no tools to prove any of this definitively — until they contacted BESITS.

The BESITS Solution

Our team conducted a site visit and assessed the fleet. We recommended and installed covert GPS trackers on all eight vehicles, connected to a cloud-based fleet management dashboard accessible from any smartphone or computer. The installation was completed discreetly over a single weekend, and the monitoring began immediately.

Within the first week, the dashboard revealed what the manager had suspected. Real-time location data showed three vehicles making unauthorised movements on evenings and weekends. Fuel consumption logs, cross-referenced with actual GPS distance data, exposed discrepancies that clearly indicated siphoning. Route playback showed systematic detours on several regular delivery routes.

"Within two weeks we had printouts of every unauthorised trip, every suspicious fuel stop, and every detour. The data was irrefutable. We couldn't argue with the GPS." — Operations Manager, Accra Logistics Client

The Results

Armed with precise, time-stamped data from the GPS system, the company took decisive action. Three drivers whose behaviour was clearly documented were dismissed following disciplinary hearings. The remaining five drivers, now aware that every journey was being recorded, changed their behaviour immediately and permanently.

Over the following twelve months, the company tracked its savings carefully:

  • Fuel costs dropped by 18%, saving approximately GHS 7,200 annually.
  • Maintenance costs fell due to lower total mileage, saving an estimated GHS 2,800.
  • Overtime and false mileage claims were eliminated, saving a further GHS 2,000.
  • Client satisfaction improved as on-time delivery rates rose above 94%.

Total documented savings: GHS 12,000 in year one — more than covering the cost of the entire GPS installation.

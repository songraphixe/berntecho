5 Reasons Every Ghanaian Home Needs a CCTV System in 2025

March 15, 2025 5 min read BESITS Team
Back to Blog

Home security in Ghana has changed dramatically over the past decade. With rising urbanisation across Greater Accra, Tema, and surrounding communities like Afienya, the demand for reliable, affordable surveillance has never been higher. A modern CCTV system is no longer a luxury reserved for banks and large businesses — it is quickly becoming the standard for homeowners who want peace of mind. Here are five reasons why installing one in 2025 is a decision you will not regret.

1. Crime Deterrence — Stop Trouble Before It Starts

The most powerful thing a CCTV camera does is something you will never see on the footage: it stops criminals from attempting the crime in the first place. Studies across Sub-Saharan Africa consistently show that properties with visible surveillance cameras are significantly less likely to be targeted by burglars, trespassers, and armed robbers.

When a would-be intruder spots a camera above your gate or on your perimeter wall, the risk-reward calculation changes immediately. For a relatively small investment, you are essentially broadcasting the message: "This property is watched." In neighbourhoods where opportunistic theft is a concern, that message alone is worth every pesewa spent.

Tip: Position at least one camera to cover your main entrance clearly and make it visible. A deterred criminal is far better than footage of one.

2. Remote Monitoring Straight From Your Phone

Modern IP CCTV systems connect directly to your smartphone via apps like Hik-Connect, DMSS, or XMEye. Whether you are at the office in Accra Central, travelling to Kumasi, or sitting in a meeting in Tema, you can pull up a live view of your compound, your children's play area, or your front gate in seconds.

This real-time visibility transforms the way you manage your household. You can verify whether a delivery arrived, check that your gate is closed, or simply confirm the children got home safely from school — all without making a single phone call. With push-notification alerts triggered by motion detection, your phone becomes an always-on security guard that never takes a day off.

3. Hard Evidence When You Need It Most

When incidents do occur — a theft, a vehicle scrape, a boundary dispute, or an assault — the police and courts need evidence. Eyewitness accounts are subjective and often contradictory. CCTV footage is objective. It shows exactly what happened, when it happened, and who was responsible.

Ghana Police Service investigators increasingly rely on residential CCTV footage to build prosecutable cases. Without footage, many petty crimes go unresolved simply because there is insufficient evidence. A properly installed system with enough storage to retain at least 30 days of recordings can make the critical difference between a case being closed and justice being served.

4. Can Reduce Your Insurance Premiums

Several insurers operating in Ghana now factor security installations into their home and contents premium calculations. A verified CCTV system, particularly when combined with an alarm and an electric fence, signals to underwriters that your property represents a lower risk of claims. Some policyholders have reported meaningful reductions in their annual premiums after upgrading their security infrastructure.

Even where a direct discount is not offered, the footage from your cameras can dramatically accelerate the claims process after a break-in or vandalism incident, reducing disputes and ensuring you receive your rightful payout without prolonged back-and-forth with assessors.

5. Protect Your Domestic Workers and Children

For families with young children, house helps, or elderly relatives at home during the day, CCTV provides an invaluable layer of accountability and care. Indoor cameras positioned in common areas (not bedrooms or bathrooms) allow you to confirm that routines are being followed, that children are safe, and that household rules are respected.

This is not about distrust — it is about responsibility. In the rare event that something goes wrong, whether a child has an accident, a dispute arises with a staff member, or an unauthorised visitor enters the home, you have a clear record. Many families also find that transparent camera placement actually improves trust between employers and domestic workers because expectations are clear for everyone.

Conclusion

A CCTV system is one of the highest-return security investments a Ghanaian homeowner can make in 2025. From deterring criminals before they act to giving you eyes on your property from anywhere in the world, the benefits are immediate and lasting. The cost of installation has dropped significantly with advances in IP camera technology, making professional-grade surveillance accessible to most household budgets.

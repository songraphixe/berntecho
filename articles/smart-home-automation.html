Smart Home Automation: Transforming Living in Ghana

January 18, 2025 7 min read BESITS Team
Back to Blog

A decade ago, "smart home" was a concept reserved for Silicon Valley executives and Hollywood movies. Today, it is a practical, affordable reality for Ghanaian homeowners — and BESITS is at the centre of making it happen across Greater Accra and beyond.

From remote-controlled gates to automated lighting and smart locks that recognise your fingerprint, connected living is here. Here is what you need to know.

What Is Smart Home Automation?

Smart home automation means connecting your home's electrical devices — lights, gates, locks, air conditioners, CCTV cameras, and more — to a central system you can control from your smartphone, tablet, or voice.

This is not just about convenience. Smart automation integrates your security, energy management, and comfort into one controllable system. You can lock your gate from Accra while you are in Kumasi. You can check your CCTV cameras on your phone while you are at work. You can schedule your lights to turn off at midnight and your air conditioner to start 30 minutes before you arrive home.

BESITS fact: We have installed smart home systems in over 200 properties across Ghana — homes, guesthouses, schools, and commercial properties.

Key Technologies We Install

1. Smart Locks & Biometric Entry

Replace your traditional key lock with a fingerprint, PIN, or card-based smart lock. Grant and revoke access remotely — perfect for homes with domestic workers, rental properties, and offices. No more lost keys, no more hiding keys under doormats.

2. Automated Gates & Barriers

A sliding or swing gate motor with GSM control allows you to open your gate from your phone or set it to open automatically when your phone is detected nearby. Guest access can be managed via a call button or intercom with camera.

3. Smart Lighting Control

Schedule lights to turn on at dusk and off at dawn. Set motion-triggered outdoor lights for security. Control bedroom lighting via app or voice. Smart bulbs and switches can be installed without rewiring your entire home.

4. CCTV Integration

Modern CCTV systems connect to your home network and stream live footage directly to your phone. With motion detection, you get instant push notifications when movement is detected — whether you are home or not.

5. Smart Sockets & Energy Monitoring

Plug-in smart sockets let you control appliances remotely and monitor how much power each device is consuming. Set schedules, get consumption reports, and reduce your electricity bill — all from your phone.

What Does It Cost in Ghana?

Smart home automation is more affordable than most people expect. A basic package for a 3-bedroom home — including a smart gate motor, 2 smart locks, and remote-access CCTV — typically starts around GHS 3,500 to GHS 6,000, including installation.

A more comprehensive system with lighting control, intercom, and energy monitoring can run from GHS 10,000 to GHS 25,000 depending on the size of the property and the brands specified. BESITS sources equipment from certified manufacturers including Hikvision, Dahua, and Yonusa — balancing quality and value for Ghana's climate and infrastructure.

Is It Worth It?

The answer is almost always yes — especially when you factor in the security benefit. A smart home is not just convenient; it is significantly harder to break into than a traditionally secured property. Combining electric fencing, biometric locks, CCTV with mobile alerts, and an automated gate creates multiple layers of deterrence that most criminals are unwilling to attempt.

For business owners, smart systems also reduce labour costs — you do not need a dedicated gate man when your gate opens automatically, and you do not need to be on-site to manage access to your offices or warehouses.

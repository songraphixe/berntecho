Network Security Best Practices for Ghanaian SMEs

December 20, 2024 4 min read BESITS Team
Back to Blog

Cybercrime targeting small and medium enterprises (SMEs) in West Africa is rising faster than many business owners realise. According to Interpol's Africa Cyberthreat Assessment Report, Ghana ranks among the top targeted countries in sub-Saharan Africa — with phishing, business email compromise (BEC), and ransomware attacks accounting for the majority of incidents.

The common misconception is that only large corporations are targeted. In reality, SMEs are often the preferred target because they store valuable customer data while typically having weaker security measures than larger organisations.

Key stat: The average cost of a cyberattack on a small business is estimated at over $25,000 in direct losses — not counting reputational damage and lost clients.

Why Ghanaian SMEs Are Targets

Several factors make Ghanaian SMEs attractive to cybercriminals:

  • Rapid adoption of digital payments and online banking without equivalent security investment
  • Widespread use of personal email accounts and shared passwords for business systems
  • Lack of dedicated IT staff to monitor networks and respond to incidents
  • Minimal employee training on recognising phishing or social engineering attacks
  • Outdated routers and network equipment with default factory passwords

6 Essential Security Practices

1 Use Strong, Unique Passwords for Everything

Default passwords on routers, cameras, and software are the number one entry point for attackers. Change every default password immediately. Use a password manager like Bitwarden or 1Password to generate and store unique, 16+ character passwords for each account. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on email, banking, and cloud storage accounts.

2 Install and Maintain a Business Firewall

A hardware firewall sits between your internet connection and your internal network, filtering malicious traffic before it reaches your computers. Consumer-grade routers are not sufficient for business use. BESITS supplies and configures business-grade firewall appliances appropriate for SME budgets — providing enterprise-level protection without enterprise-level cost.

3 Separate Your Networks

Your business computers, CCTV cameras, and customer WiFi should all be on separate network segments (VLANs). If a guest or customer device is compromised, it cannot reach your accounting software or internal files. This simple configuration is one of the most effective and underused security measures for Ghanaian businesses.

4 Back Up Regularly — and Test Your Backups

Ransomware attacks encrypt your files and demand payment to restore access. The only reliable defence is a current, tested backup stored separately from your main systems. Follow the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies of data, on 2 different storage types, with 1 copy offsite (or in the cloud). Test your restore process at least once every quarter.

5 Train Your Team to Recognise Phishing

Over 90% of successful cyberattacks begin with a phishing email — a convincing message that tricks a staff member into clicking a malicious link or providing login credentials. Run regular short training sessions. Teach employees to verify sender email addresses carefully, never click unexpected links, and report suspicious messages before taking action.

6 Get Professional IT Support

The most cost-effective security decision most SMEs can make is engaging a professional IT partner for ongoing monitoring and maintenance.

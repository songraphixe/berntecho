Off-Grid Solar: The Ultimate Solution for Ghana's Power Cuts

January 5, 2025 5 min read BESITS Team
Back to Blog

Load-shedding — locally known as "dumsor" — is a reality that millions of Ghanaians live with every week. ECG schedules can be unpredictable, outages can last for hours, and the consequences for homes and businesses range from spoiled food to failed security systems to lost business hours.

An off-grid solar system changes all of this. Once installed, your home or office runs on clean, reliable solar power — independent of the national grid. Whether ECG is on or off, your lights stay on, your CCTV keeps recording, and your refrigerator keeps running.

The Power Cut Reality in Ghana

Ghana's electricity challenges are well-documented. Despite significant investment in generation capacity, distribution failures and seasonal shortfalls mean that power cuts remain part of daily life — particularly in suburban and peri-urban areas like Tema, Afienya, Kasoa, and Ashaiman.

For businesses, every hour of downtime costs money. For homes with security systems, a power cut is precisely when your CCTV, electric fence, and alarm become vulnerable — unless you have a backup power solution.

Solar fact: Ghana receives an average of 4.5–6 peak sun hours per day — among the highest in the world. An off-grid solar system here is one of the most cost-effective in Africa.

How Off-Grid Solar Works

An off-grid solar system has four main components working together:

  • Solar panels: Capture sunlight and convert it to DC electricity
  • Charge controller: Regulates the current flowing into your batteries to prevent overcharging
  • Battery bank: Stores energy generated during the day for use at night or during cloudy periods
  • Inverter: Converts stored DC power into the AC power your home appliances use

A hybrid system adds a grid connection, allowing you to draw from ECG when solar generation is insufficient and export surplus power when the sun is strong. BESITS installs both pure off-grid and hybrid systems depending on your location and needs.

What Components You Need

System size depends entirely on your load — what appliances you want to power and for how long. A typical Ghanaian 3-bedroom home running lights, fans, phone charging, and a small television needs a system roughly in this range:

  • 4 × 330W solar panels (1.32kW total)
  • 2 × 200Ah lithium or gel batteries
  • 1.5–3kVA hybrid inverter with MPPT charge controller
  • Mounting frame, wiring, and protection devices

To also power an air conditioner, water pump, or refrigerator, system capacity needs to increase accordingly. BESITS conducts a load assessment for every installation — we size your system correctly from the start so you are never under or over-invested.

What It Costs (Rough Estimates)

Solar system prices vary based on components, brand, and system size. As a rough guide for Ghana:

  • Basic home system (lights, fans, TV): GHS 8,000 – 14,000 installed
  • Mid-range system (+ fridge, office equipment): GHS 18,000 – 28,000 installed
  • Full home system (+ AC, water pump): GHS 35,000 – 65,000+ installed

Most systems pay for themselves within 3–5 years through eliminated electricity bills — after which the energy is essentially free for the remaining 20+ year lifespan of the panels.
